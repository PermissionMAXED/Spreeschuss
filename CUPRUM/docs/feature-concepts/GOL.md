# Family GOL — Golemcraft & Automation Programming (`golemcraft`)

Global sequence 123–142 · 20 features (16 core / 4 stretch) · core wave W9, stretch W15 · catalog family string: `golemcraft`

Programs, routines and infrastructure for U12. Scope split: **U12 is the programmable-golem system itself** (entity capability, task executor, base AI); **GOL-01 is the distinct survival acquisition path** — the craftable Brass Cortex item and its conversion ritual. Programs are data-driven op graphs (move/scan/take/put/if/wait/signal), never arbitrary code.

**Security contract (binding):** program uploads are C2S payloads capped at 4 KiB and 32 ops, rate-limited to 1 upload/s per player, validated server-side (op whitelist, area bounds ≤48³ per program, container access only in chunks the owner may edit); golems act with their owner's block-edit permissions, never more; GOL-16 purchases are rate-limited to 4/s per player and settle atomically.
**Balance constants:** one automaton ≈ the throughput of a simple hopper line but flexible; task ops cost golem charge (walk 1 Cg/block, block action 5 Cg) so perches and PWR supply gate fleet size.
**Performance budget:** ≤0.08 ms/tick per active automaton; pathing reuses the waypoint graph with cached routes; docked golems tick at 1/10 rate.
**Persistence contract:** program, program counter, inventory and charge persist in entity NBT; after chunk reload or restart a golem resumes at the exact op index it was executing.
**Cross-family interfaces:** routines read/write TUB ports and chests; GOL-06 requires U15 detector marks; GOL-03 punch cards are also readable by DEC-07 carillons as note scores (DEC is a later wave); TES-05 Arc Welder repairs golems; MOB-09 rogue units are hacked via GOL-12.
